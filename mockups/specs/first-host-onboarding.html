Neotilde — First-host onboarding & Tips & Gestures

No forced walkthrough, no JIT tooltips. Empty state has one primary action and two secondary links: Settings and Tips & Gestures. The Esc-pill picker gains a permanent Tips & Gestures row. Both lead to the same scrollable reference screen.

First launch. Centered "Add your first host" CTA, one line of micro-copy that sets form expectations, and a quiet row of secondary links beneath. Keybar is hidden — it has nothing to act on yet.

3 — Tips & Gestures screen — on-device

Single scrollable screen. Six sections, each with prose + a small static diagram. Top-anchored close button. Same content from either entry point.

The keybar

Locked-left for muscle memory; scroll region for context. Tap a key for the primary char; swipe up/down for the dim secondaries.

The Esc pill

Tap to send Escape. Long-press to open the picker — hosts, Settings, and this screen.

The Pad

Drag for arrow keys (mouse-like, not joystick). Tap to zoom on the cursor. Long-press to arm split mode.

Context-aware promotions

When the foreground process is vim, less, python, psql… related symbols promote into the scroll region with bronze tint.

Scrolling reference, top-anchored close. Six sections total: Keybar, Esc pill, Pad, Context promotions, Modifiers, Fn keys. No animation, no autoplay, no localisation in v1. Returning to this screen at any time costs nothing — no state stored.

Sections, in order

  1. The keybar — locked vs scroll regions, tap/swipe vocabulary.
  2. The Esc pill — tap = Esc, long-press = picker.
  3. The Pad — drag/tap/long-press semantics.
  4. Context-aware promotions — symbols change with foreground process.
  5. Modifiers — sticky-for-one; double-tap Ctrl to lock.
  6. Fn keys — Fn slot toggles F1–F12; auto-engages in htop/top/mc.

Section order = roughly the order a user encounters the surface. The keybar orientation paragraph subsumes a separate "swipe-up/down secondary chars" tooltip (cut as self-explanatory from the dim glyphs on each key).

Each section uses prose + one static SVG diagram. No animated callouts, no looping clips, no per-section tracking.

4 — Out of scope (explicit)

What we are not building, and why these were ruled out during brainstorm:

  • JIT tooltips on first encounter — replaced by voluntary reference. No coach marks, spotlight overlays, animated callouts.
  • Demo PTY / sample session — adds a subsystem for marginal first-impression value.
  • Prefilled example host — creates a "what is this row?" confusion moment.
  • Multi-page swipe tour — feels like a forced walkthrough even when voluntary.
  • "Unread" badge / read-state tracking — symmetrical entry points, no analytics, no sync.
  • Localisation — English only for v1, matching the rest of the app.
  • Predictor / keybar-swipe explainers — judged self-explanatory; covered (if at all) by the keybar orientation paragraph.
