Glymr — Host management & settings access

How the user reaches the host picker, switches between live connections, edits hosts, and opens Settings. Colors and components come from design-system.html.

Entry point — long-press Esc

No host chip. No top bar. No menu slot. The picker (which contains live hosts, recents, "+ Connect," and "⚙ Settings") is reached by long-pressing the Esc slot in the keybar. A small dim ≡ glyph appears below "Esc" as a discoverability hint. Esc is a core/locked slot whose long-press was previously undefined — no overload, no conflict, no new chrome.

Picker sheet opens. Anchored above the keybar (where Esc is) rather than at the top, so the user's eyes stay near where their thumb just was. Live hosts → recents → Connect → Settings.

What's locked:
  • Entry point: long-press Esc slot in the keybar (~400ms threshold).
  • Hint glyph: small dim bronze ≡ below the "Esc" label.
  • Engage feedback: Esc slot highlights bronze + scales slightly + light haptic on engage.
  • Picker anchor: opens just above the keybar (near where the gesture happened), not at the top of screen.
  • Picker contents: Live hosts → Recents → "+ Connect to host…" → "⚙ Settings."
  • No host chip anywhere. Host identity comes from the shell prompt; the picker itself shows current host highlighted.
  • No top bar. Terminal extends to DI safe-area edge. Status banners still slide in from the top when needed (unchanged from the banner spec).
  • Esc tap behavior unchanged. Single tap = fire Esc key. Only long-press is overloaded.
Open questions still to resolve:
  • What counts as "live" — strictly mosh-roaming, or also SSH idle?
  • Per-row swipe actions in picker — disconnect, edit, copy hostname?
  • Banner ↔ keyboard interaction — does the banner still appear at the very top of the screen, or above the keybar near the picker?
  • Then the meaty stuff: host config model, CRUD flow, multi-conn switching semantics.
